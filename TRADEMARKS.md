# Azents Trademark Policy

This policy explains how the Azents name, logos, icons, and other brand assets may be used.

The Azents source code is licensed under the MIT License. The MIT License does not grant any trademark rights. The Azents name, logo, and brand assets remain trademarks or trade dress of the Azents project and its maintainers.

## Goals

We want the Azents software to be easy to use, fork, modify, distribute, and self-host. We also want users to clearly understand when a product or service is official, endorsed, or operated by the Azents project.

In short: you may truthfully describe your relationship to Azents, but you may not make your project, product, or service look like the official Azents project or an official Azents provider without written permission.

## Permitted uses

You may use the Azents name without prior permission when the use is truthful, non-misleading, and does not imply official endorsement. For example, you may:

- say that your project is built with Azents, based on Azents, powered by Azents, compatible with Azents, or a fork of Azents;
- refer to Azents in documentation, tutorials, blog posts, talks, books, benchmarks, and comparison materials;
- use the Azents name to identify compatibility with the Azents APIs, formats, or deployment model;
- use the Azents name in package metadata, dependency lists, or attribution notices where needed to identify the upstream project;
- use unmodified Azents screenshots for non-commercial documentation, reviews, or educational content, as long as the context is clear.

## Uses that require written permission

You may not use the Azents name, logos, icons, or brand assets in a way that suggests you are the official Azents project, an official Azents cloud or hosting provider, or endorsed by Azents unless you have written permission from the Azents maintainers.

Examples of prohibited uses include:

- naming your product, company, domain, or hosted service `Azents`, `Azents Cloud`, `Azents Hosting`, `Azents Managed Service`, or a confusingly similar name;
- claiming to be the official Azents service, official Azents cloud, official Azents provider, or certified Azents provider;
- using the Azents logo as your product logo, company logo, application icon, or primary branding;
- using Azents brand assets in a way that makes your fork, distribution, integration, or hosted service appear to be operated by Azents;
- registering domains, social media handles, app store listings, package names, or marketplace listings that imply official Azents ownership or endorsement;
- removing, obscuring, or replacing Azents attribution in a way that misleads users about the origin of the software.

## Forks and modified versions

You may fork and modify Azents under the MIT License. If you distribute a modified version, make it clear that it is not the official Azents project unless you have written permission.

Suggested naming patterns:

- `ExampleAgent, a fork of Azents`
- `ExampleAgent, built with Azents`
- `ExampleAgent, compatible with Azents`

Avoid names and presentation that imply official status, such as:

- `Azents Pro`
- `Azents Cloud`
- `Official Azents Hosting`
- `Azents Enterprise by ExampleCorp`

## Hosted and managed services

The MIT License allows you to operate hosted or managed services based on Azents. This trademark policy does not prohibit that. However, your service must not imply that it is official, endorsed, certified, or operated by Azents unless you have written permission.

Acceptable examples:

- `ExampleCorp Agent Cloud, powered by Azents`
- `Managed hosting for Azents by ExampleCorp`
- `ExampleCorp Azents-compatible platform`

Prohibited examples without permission:

- `Azents Cloud`
- `Official Azents Cloud`
- `Azents Managed Service`
- `Certified Azents Hosting`

## Brand assets

Do not use Azents logos, icons, or other brand assets as your own primary branding. Limited use for truthful references, documentation, or attribution is acceptable when it is clear that your project or service is independent from Azents.

If you need to use Azents brand assets in a way not covered by this policy, ask for permission first.

## Questions and permission requests

If you are unsure whether a use is permitted, or if you want permission for a use that is not covered here, contact the Azents maintainers before using the Azents marks.

The maintainers may update this policy over time to clarify permitted and prohibited uses.
